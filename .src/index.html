Did You Know?

Random finance facts to impress your study group — or just yourself.

🧠
Fun Fact #01

The New York Stock Exchange was founded under a buttonwood tree in 1792. The original agreement was signed by just 24 stockbrokers.

💸
Fun Fact #02

The term "bull market" comes from the way a bull attacks — thrusting its horns upward. A "bear market" refers to a bear swiping its paws downward.

🏦
Fun Fact #03

The world's oldest bank still in operation is Banca Monte dei Paschi di Siena, founded in 1472 in Italy. That's over 550 years of banking!

🎲
Fun Fact #04

The concept of "risk" in finance comes from the Italian word "risicare," meaning "to dare." Every investment is essentially a dare against uncertainty.

📉
Fun Fact #05

The 1929 stock market crash led to the creation of the SEC. Before that, insider trading was basically legal — and wildly common on Wall Street.

🌍
Fun Fact #06

The global derivatives market is estimated to be worth over $600 trillion. That's roughly 6 times the size of the entire world's GDP.
