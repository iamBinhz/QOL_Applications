## Slide 62

16.4  PRICE DISCRIMINATION
Price Discrimination and Consumer Surplus
The key idea behind price discrimination is to convert consumer surplus into economic profit.
To extract every dollar of consumer surplus from every buyer, the monopoly would have to offer each individual customer a separate price schedule based on that customer’s own willingness to pay.

Price Discrimination and Efficiency
With perfect price discrimination, price equals marginal cost, so deadweight loss is zero.
Perfect price discrimination redistributes the consumer surplus to the producer. Consumer surplus is zero.

## Slide 90

Fixed Cost
The marginal cost of a copy of Windows might be close to zero, but the fixed cost of developing the software is large.
Microsoft must earn at least enough revenue to pay these fixed costs.
Earning enough to pay the firm’s fixed costs does not inevitably lead to inefficiency.
Some firms with zero marginal cost and the market power to charge a high price choose to provide the efficient quantity of their services at a zero price.
Google is one such firm.

The price of an Internet search on Google is zero.
