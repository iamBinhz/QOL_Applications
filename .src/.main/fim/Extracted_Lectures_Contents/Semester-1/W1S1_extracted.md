Importance of Financial Markets 
• Financial markets are critical for an efficient allocation of 
funds, allowing funds to move from people who lack 
productive investment opportunities to people who have 
them . 
• Financial markets also improve the well-being of 
consumers, allowing them to time their purchases better. 
Structure of Financial Markets 
1. Debt Markets 
– Pay interest 
– Products are loans (government bonds, corporate 
bonds…etc) 
2. Equity Markets 
– Pay dividends 
– Products are shares and represent an ownership claim 
in the firm (stock market). 
Structure of Financial Markets 
1. Primary Market 
– New security issues sold to initial buyers 
– Typically involves an investment bank who underwrites 
the offering 
2. Secondary Market 
– Securities previously issued are bought and sold 
e.g., London Stock Exchange, New York Stock Exchange, etc.

Function of Financial Intermediaries: 
Indirect Finance 
• In major developed countries (The US, the UK, Canada, Japan, Italy, 
Germany, and France), majority of business funding is indirect (through 
financial intermediaries) 
• Financial intermediaries are preferred because of 
– Lower transaction costs, 
– risk sharing, 
– asymmetric information 
We will further explore these functions and benefits NEXT WEEK. 
Side note: Assets and Liabilities 
• An asset is a resource with economic value with the expectation that it will 
provide a future benefit 
– Money in the bank, property, car, machinery….. 
• Main types are short-term assets, long-term (fixed) assets, financial assets 
and intangible assets 
• A liability is a resource that is owed to somebody else 
– Loans, etc 
• Main types of liabilities are short term (to be paid in less than a year) and long- 
term liabilities (longer than a year).

4. Types of Financial Intermediaries 
Depository Institutions (BANKS) accept deposits and make loans. 
1. Commercial banks 
– Raise funds primarily through current and savings account deposits, 
which are used to make commercial, consumer and mortgage loans 
– Commercial banks comprise the largest financial intermediary and have 
the most diversified asset portfolios 
2. Building Societies (in the UK), thrifts, savings & loan associations in the US, 
credit unions also raise funds primarily collecting current deposits which are 
most often used to make mortgage and consumer loans, 
– They issue deposits as shares and are owned collectively by their 
depositors .

4. Types of Financial Intermediaries 
Non-depository institutions (NON-BANKS) come in two types: 
2. Investment Companies 
– Unit trusts in the UK/ Mutual Funds in the US: acquire funds by selling shares to 
individual investors and use the proceeds to purchase large, diversified portfolios 
of stocks and bonds 
– Hedge Funds are a type of mutual fund requiring large investments, long holding 
periods, and are subject to few regulations 
– Investment Banks (sometimes referred as Merchant Banks in the UK): advise 
companies on securities to issue, underwriting security offerings, offer M&A 
assistance, and act as dealers in security markets.
