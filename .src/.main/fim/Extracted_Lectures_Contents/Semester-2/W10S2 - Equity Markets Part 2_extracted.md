London Stock Exchange 
1) Main Market : is the primary market for trading equities of large 
and established companies. It is divided into segments such as the 
Premium Segment and the Standard Segment, which have different 
listing requirements based on market capitalization, corporate 
governance standards, and history of financial performance.

environment compared to the Main Market. 
3) Specialist Markets : caters to specific types of securities and 
trading activities such as trading fixed - income securities, exchange - 
traded funds (ETFs), derivatives, and international securities.

Stock Market Indexes 
• The Dow Jones Industrial Average (the Dow) is a price - weighted 
index which means that stocks with higher prices have more influence 
on the index's value. 
• It is the oldest index in use today (introduced in 1896). 
• It is calculated by adding up the prices of 30 component stocks and 
dividing the sum by the divisor. 
• The divisor is adjusted periodically to account for stock splits, 
dividends, and other factors to ensure continuity in the index value 
over time.

Stock Market Impact on the Economy 
• Wealth Effect : A stock market crash can lead to a substantial decrease 
in household wealth, as the value of investment portfolios declines. 
Leads to reduced consumer confidence and spending. 
• Investment and Capital Spending : Corporations often rely on equity 
markets for financing through stock issuance. A stock market crash 
can hinder their ability to raise capital – can lower investment and 
slow down economic growth. 
• Credit and Lending : A sharp decline in stock prices can erode the 
value of collateral held by financial institutions, leading to tighter 
credit conditions. This reduce lending and dampen economic activity.

## Page 27

Stock Market Impact on the Economy 
• Business Confidence and employment : Stock market downturn can 
signal economic uncertainty - lead businesses to delay hiring 
decisions and investment plans. This can result in higher 
unemployment and lower consumer spending. 
• Impact on Government Finances : Governments may experience 
reduced tax revenues during economic downturns (due to lower 
corporate profits and individual income). Governments may need to 
increase spending to support social welfare and stimulate economy. 
• Global Spillover Effects : Stock market crashes can have spillover 
effects beyond national borders. Declines in one country's stock 
market can trigger sell - offs in other markets.
