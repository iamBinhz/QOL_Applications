## Page 20

General and specific rates of interest 
• For simplicity, we often talk about the interest rate as though it was a 
single entity. 
• In fact, there are lots of different interest rates, with different lenders 
charging different rates for different loan arrangements. 
• We need, therefore, to distinguish between the general and the 
specific rate of interest. 
• In effect, the general rate is just the average of all the prevailing 
specific rates.

6 - The Term Structure of Interest Rates 
• The rate of interest varies between loans according to 
their durations. 
• Typically, the longer the loan duration the higher the rate 
of interest charged. 
• This is because the risks associated with lending increase 
with time, so the rate of interest must also increase. 
• The relationship between loan duration and rate of 
interest is described by the yield curve.

## Page 35

7 - Expectations and the term structure of interest 
rates 
• Although the yield curve typically slopes up from left to right, 
it can slope downwards or even go up before turning down 
again. 
• This is because the yield curve is telling us what lenders 
currently expect to happen in the future. 
• If, for example, lenders expect inflation to fall in the long 
term, the yield curve will rise over the short and medium 
terms, but turn down in the long term
