## WEEK 1: Financial Systems Fundamentals

### Key Concepts
1. **Primary Role of Financial System**: Channeling funds from savers (surplus units) to borrowers (deficit units)
2. **Capital Markets**: Deal with long-term debt and equity instruments
3. **Primary vs Secondary Markets**:
   - Primary: New securities issued, raises new funds
   - Secondary: Existing securities traded, provides liquidity
4. **Direct vs Indirect Finance**:
   - Direct: Borrowers sell securities directly to savers
   - Indirect: Funds flow through financial intermediaries

### Financial Intermediaries
| Type | Examples | Primary Source of Funds |
|------|----------|------------------------|
| Depository | Commercial banks, Credit unions, Savings banks | Deposits |
| Contractual Savings | Life insurance, Pension funds | Premiums, Contributions |
| Investment | Mutual funds, Hedge funds | Share sales |

### Bank vs Non-Bank Balance Sheets
- **Banks**: Loans as assets, deposits as liabilities
- **Non-bank FIs**: No deposits; use insurance/pension obligations as liabilities

## WEEK 2: Why Financial Intermediaries?

### Transaction Costs
- Definition: Time and money spent carrying out financial transactions
- **Economies of Scale**: Spreading costs over many borrowers reduces per-transaction cost
- Example: Standardized loan contracts

### Asymmetric Information Problems

| Problem | When It Occurs | Definition |
|---------|----------------|------------|
| **Adverse Selection** | BEFORE transaction | Bad credit risks most actively seek loans |
| **Moral Hazard** | AFTER transaction | Borrower changes behavior after getting funds |

### The Lemons Problem
- Good securities undervalued, bad securities overvalued
- Buyers can't distinguish quality, so they discount everything

### Solutions to Asymmetric Information
1. Government regulation (disclosure requirements)
2. Financial intermediation (screening expertise)
3. Collateral requirements
4. **NOT**: Increasing interest rates (worsens adverse selection)

### Principal-Agent Problem
- Agents act in their own interest rather than principals'

### Debt vs Equity Markets
| Feature | Debt | Equity |
|---------|------|--------|
| Claim type | Fixed (interest payments) | Residual (profits after all claims paid) |
| Ownership | No | Yes |
| Maturity | Short-term (<1 year) or Long-term | Perpetual |

## WEEK 3: How Banks Create Money

### Money Supply Definitions
| Measure | Components |
|---------|------------|
| **M0 (Narrow)** | Cash in circulation + Reserve balances at BoE |
| **M4 (Broad)** | M0 + Savings deposits + Time deposits |

### Key Fact
- Only ~3% of circulating money is physical cash (banknotes/coins)
- ~97% is bank deposits (digital money)

### Bank Balance Sheet
| Assets | Liabilities |
|--------|-------------|
| Loans | Customer deposits |
| Securities | Borrowings |
| Reserves | Equity |
| Real estate | |

### How Banks Create Money
1. Bank makes a loan
2. Loan amount credited to borrower's deposit account
3. Money supply INCREASES
4. Process repeats as deposits are re-lent

### Money Multiplier
```
Simple Deposit Multiplier = 1 / Required Reserve Ratio

Example: If RRR = 10%, multiplier = 1/0.10 = 10
Example: If RRR = 15%, multiplier = 1/0.15 = 6.67
```

### Factors That Reduce Money Expansion
- Increase in currency holdings (people hold cash instead of deposits)
- Increase in excess reserves (banks hold more than required)

### Monetary Base
```
Monetary Base = Currency in Circulation + Reserves
```
Both are **central bank liabilities**

### Bank of England Responsibilities
- Conduct monetary policy
- Supervise banking system
- Issue national currency
- **NOT**: Setting corporate tax rates

## WEEK 4: UK Banking System Structure

### Building Societies
- Began in **1775** in UK
- Original purpose: Finance house building for members
- **1980s**: Allowed to demutualize and become limited companies
- Today: ~13% of outstanding residential mortgages
- Largest: **Nationwide** (holds >60% of building society assets)

### Market Concentration
**Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)**
```
HHI = Sum of (Market Share)^2 for all firms

Example: Banks with 30%, 25%, 20%, 15%, 10%
HHI = 30² + 25² + 20² + 15² + 10² = 900 + 625 + 400 + 225 + 100 = 2,250
```

| HHI Range | Concentration Level |
|-----------|---------------------|
| < 1,500 | Un-concentrated |
| 1,500 - 2,500 | Moderately concentrated |
| > 2,500 | Highly concentrated |
| 10,000 | Perfect monopoly |

### Why UK Banking is Concentrated
1. Very low switching rates (customer inertia)
2. Economies of scale favor large banks
3. Regulation favors larger banks
4. Increasing product complexity

### Challenger Banks
- Definition: Small, recently created retail banks competing with large banks
- Examples: **Starling, Monzo, Metro**
- NOT Barclays (traditional large bank)
- Generally have higher customer satisfaction

### Open Banking
- Definition: Banks share customer data with app developers and other institutions
- Goal: Increase competition and innovation
- Benefits: Consumers and fintech companies
- Risks: Security risks for customer data, may "backfire"

## WEEK 5: Various Banking Systems

### Universal Banking (German Model)
- Integration of commercial AND investment banking
- Large equity stakes in industrial companies
- Advantages: One-stop shopping, lower transaction costs
- Disadvantages: Complex risk management, conflicts of interest

### US Banking Evolution

| Period | Characteristic |
|--------|----------------|
| Pre-1933 | No separation |
| 1933-1999 | **Glass-Steagall Act** - Strict separation of commercial/investment banking |
| 1999+ | Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act - Reversal, Bank Holding Companies formed |
| 2010+ | **Dodd-Frank Act** - Response to GFC, stricter regulation |

### US Banking Post-GFC Changes
- Increased capital ratios
- Reduced proprietary trading
- Less reliance on short-term wholesale funding
- COVID-19 response: Maintained stability, accelerated digital banking

### Deposit Insurance
| Country | Coverage |
|---------|----------|
| USA | First to introduce (1933), currently $250,000 |
| UK | £85,000 per depositor per institution |

**Moral Hazard of Deposit Insurance**: Depositors don't monitor bank risk-taking

### US Reserve Requirements
- Since March 2020: **0%** (reduced to support economy during COVID)

### Ring-Fencing
- Purpose: Separate retail banking from investment banking
- Protects retail customers from investment banking risks
- UK: Banking Reform Act 2013

### Chinese Banking System
- **1949**: Nationalization into monobank (PBoC)
- **1980s**: "Big Four" state-owned commercial banks established
- Characteristics: State ownership of major banks
- Expansion: Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) cross-border lending
- Downsides: Difficulty for small businesses to get loans, NPLs from state-owned enterprise lending
- Shadow banking: Primarily serves small businesses

## WEEK 6: Investment Banking

### Core Functions
| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Underwriting** | Purchase and resell new securities to investors (IPOs) |
| **Market Making** | Provide liquidity by buying/selling securities |
| **Equity Research** | Produce reports with investment recommendations |
| **Asset Management** | Earn management fees and portfolio commissions |
| **Proprietary Trading** | Trade bank's own funds for profit |

### Trading Concepts
- **Bid-Ask Spread**: Difference between bid and ask price
- **Front-Running**: Trading ahead of client orders for profit (UNETHICAL)

### Chinese Wall
- Barrier separating advisory and trading teams to prevent conflicts of interest

### Proprietary Trading Risks
- Conflicts of interest
- High financial exposure
- **Volcker Rule**: Prevents proprietary trading by commercial banks

### Ring-Fencing in UK
- **Banking Reform Act 2013**
- Separates retail banking from investment banking
- Purpose: Protect retail customers from investment banking risks
- Criticism: Limits competitiveness and flexibility

### Key Investment Bank History
- **JP Morgan**: Merger with Chase Manhattan in **2000** to form JPMorgan Chase
- **Morgan Stanley**: Created 1935 due to **Glass-Steagall Act**
- **Goldman Sachs**: First IPO managed in 1906 (Sears, Roebuck and Co.); converted to bank holding company in **2008**

### Investment Bank Funding
- Primary: Wholesale market borrowing and repo agreements
- NOT: Retail customer deposits

## WEEK 7/11: Islamic Banking

### Core Prohibitions
| Arabic Term | Meaning | Why Prohibited |
|-------------|---------|----------------|
| **Riba** | Interest (making money from money) | Exploitative, unearned income |
| **Gharar** | Uncertainty/speculation | Creates unfair contracts |
| **Maysir** | Gambling | Harm without productive value |

### Sharia-Compliant Investments
- ALLOWED: Building and construction
- NOT ALLOWED: Gambling, alcohol, conventional banking, weapons

### Key Principles
1. **Profit-Loss Sharing**: Risk and return shared between parties
2. **Asset-Backed**: Every deal must have underlying real asset
3. **Ethical Investment**: Socially responsible investments prioritized
4. **Equity-Oriented**: More equity-based than debt-based

### Islamic Financial Instruments

| Instrument | Description | Conventional Equivalent |
|------------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Musharaka** | Joint venture, profit-loss sharing | Partnership |
| **Mudarabah** | Profit-sharing partnership (one provides capital, other manages) | Silent partnership |
| **Murabaha** | Cost-plus sale (disclosed markup) | Interest-based loan |
| **Sukuk** | Investment certificate | Bonds |
| **Takaful** | Mutual insurance | Conventional insurance |
| **Ijara** | Lease-to-own | Finance lease |

### Islamic Social Finance
| Instrument | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| **Zakat** | Compulsory wealth redistribution (2.5% of savings) |
| **Waqf** | Charitable endowment |
| **Qard Hassan** | Interest-free loan |
| **NOT included**: Takaful (that's insurance) |

### Key Facts
- Principles based on **Qur'an and Sunnah**
- Size dominated by **banking assets** (not sukuk or takaful)
- Increases **financial inclusion**
- **2008 GFC Resilience**: Avoided toxic assets due to asset-backing requirement, no excessive leverage

### Islamic Microfinance
- Provides financing to financially excluded populations in affordable manner

## SEMESTER 2: Equity Markets

### Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)
- Current price **fully reflects all available relevant information**
- Implication: Future price changes are **unpredictable**
- Unexploited profit opportunities quickly eliminated
- If news is public, price already adjusted (no above-average returns)

### Stock Exchanges

| Exchange | Key Characteristics |
|----------|---------------------|
| **NYSE** | Largest US exchange, auction market, established 1817 |
| **NASDAQ** | Electronic exchange, known for tech companies, less stringent listing requirements, founded 1971 |
| **LSE** | Has Alternative Investment Market (AIM) for smaller/growing companies (since 1995) |

### Market Indices

| Index | Calculation Method | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------|
| **Dow Jones Industrial Average** | Price-weighted | 30 largest US companies |
| **S&P 500** | Market-cap-weighted | 500 companies |
| **FTSE 100** | Market-cap-weighted | UK's main index |

**NOT a US index**: FTSE 100 (UK)

### Stock Valuation Concepts
- **P/E Ratio** = Market Price per Share / Earnings per Share
- High P/E: Investors optimistic about future earnings
- **One-Period Valuation**: Present value of dividends AND expected sales price

### Key Theories
- **Speculative Bubble**: Prices move away from intrinsic value
- **Keynes's "Biggest Fool" Theory**: Buy because others will buy
- **Minsky's Financial Instability Hypothesis**: Explains boom and bust cycles
- **Equity Risk Premium**: Return on stocks MINUS risk-free rate

### Shareholder Types
| Type | Voting Rights | Dividend Priority | Residual Claim |
|------|--------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Common | Yes | Lower | Yes (after all other claims) |
| Preferred | No | Higher (fixed) | No |

## KEY FORMULAS

### Money Multiplier
```
Simple Deposit Multiplier = 1 / Required Reserve Ratio
Money Multiplier = Money Supply Change / Monetary Base Change
```

### Herfindahl-Hirschman Index
```
HHI = Σ(Market Share %)²

Example: 35% + 30% + 20% + 15%
HHI = 35² + 30² + 20² + 15² = 1225 + 900 + 400 + 225 = 2,750
→ Highly concentrated (>2,500)
```

### P/E Ratio
```
P/E Ratio = Market Price per Share / Earnings per Share
```

### One-Period Stock Valuation
```
P₀ = (D₁ + P₁) / (1 + r)

Where:
P₀ = Current price
D₁ = Expected dividend
P₁ = Expected selling price
r = Required return
```
