### Slide 14

Limitations of WACC Assumes constant capital structure (financing mix may change over time) Relies on market values (e.g., private firms) Ignores project-specific risk (reflects firm-wide average, not individual investment risk) Assumes perfect capital markets (no bankruptcy costs, no friction) Not valid for firms with multiple business units (WACC may not reflect the risk profile of diversified operations)

### Slide 22

Key Takeaways The cost of capital reflects the return expected by investors. WACC combines the cost of debt and equity into one overall rate. A firm’s capital structure directly influences its WACC and value. The trade-off theory weighs tax benefits of debt against financial risk. The pecking order theory explains firms financing preferences. Financial leverage can boost returns but also increases risk. Real-world capital structure decisions adapt theory to practical needs.
