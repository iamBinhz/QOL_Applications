### Slide 11

Bond A bond is a marketable fixed-income instrument that represents a loan between a borrower (issuer) and a lender (investors). A bond may also be referred to as a “loan note”, “marketable debt”, “debenture”. A government bond is issued by governments to support their spending, a corporate bond is a bond issued by a corporation to finance their operations. Bonds are first issued in the primary market through a public offering . Bondholders can then sell bonds to other investors in the secondary market. Each bond issue specifies the terms of the bond. 11

Straight Bond The market price of the bond is quoted per the face value of the bond The face value of the bond is normally $100 or $1,000 Interest is calculated as a percentage of the face value Straight bond example: A 4.375% bond is trading at $98. This means that an investor would be willing to pay $98 for a block of debt with a face value of $100 The investor would receive 4.375% x $100 interest per year. The interest on a bond is called its coupon. Therefore the coupon would be $4.375 each year 13

### Slide 17

Convertible Bond A type of bond which provides the holder the option to convert the debt into ordinary shares at a future date or have the bond redeemed for a cash lump sum . Interest is paid up to the date of conversion/redemption. Convertible bond example: A company has 5% convertible bonds, convertible in 5 years time into 50 ordinary shares or they can be r edeemed at face value of $100. So investor receives $5 interest for 5 years In 5 years time may redeem $100 OR Convert each $100 of debt into 50 ordinary shares. The shares are worth $2.50 each in 5 years time In this case the investor is likely to convert the bond into ordinary shares as the conversion will be worth $125 vs the $100 face value 17
