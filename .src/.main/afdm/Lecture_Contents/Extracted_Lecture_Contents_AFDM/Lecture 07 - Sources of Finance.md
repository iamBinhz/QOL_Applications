### Slide 13

Debt Debt finance is where an investor receives a guaranteed return each year on the capital invested, normally in the form of interest payments. A company must pay interest to the providers of debt finance BEFORE paying dividends to preference shareholders. The interest payable is tax deductible. Main types of finance to be aware of are: Bank Loan Leasing Straight bond Convertible bond Zero coupon bond Unsecured bond 15

---

### Slide 14

Equity Equity represents an ownership position in a corporation, ordinary shareholders have the right to vote on major decisions. Shareholders receive returns in the form of dividends (if declared) and capital gains (change in share price on resale). Unlike debt, dividends are not guaranteed and are paid only after all obligations to debt holders and preference shareholders have been met. Equity capital is the ordinary share capital in the business – finance raised from the ‘ordinary share holders’. Equity financing refers to raising fresh capital by selling shares of the company. Main types of equity finance to be aware of are: Retained earnings – CFOs first pick when it comes to financing projects Issue of new shares to new investors An offer for sale – IPO An offer for sale by tender A placing A rights issue 16

### Slide 15

Capital Structure – Equity Advantages Although least favourite, equity is advantageous as: No repayment of principal is required No extra burden is borne, increasing equity does not put the company at risk of bankruptcy. It reduces the company’s gearing level No mandatory periodic payment, which means more capital is free to be invested Risk is shared, shareholders share risk with the company and only receive dividends when the company generate positive earnings. Risk to  the shareholder is limited 17
