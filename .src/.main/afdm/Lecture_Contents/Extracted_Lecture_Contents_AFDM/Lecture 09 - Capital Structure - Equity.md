### Slide 6

Types of Equity Finance Retained Earnings where directors of the company decide to retain and reinvest profits rather than distribute as dividends. Simplest form of equity finance – avoids issue costs Issue of new shares to new investors The directors of the company offer new shares to new investors via: An offer for sale –IPO, Co. sets an issue price and invites public to buy shares issued that price An offer for sale by tender – a minimum price for shares is set and investors bid for the shares A placing – shares are sold or ‘placed’ by a merchant bank directly with an institutional investor or a group e.g. a large pension fund Rights issue The existing shareholders are offered the right to buy new shares in proportion to their existing shareholding Shareholders can take up the rights, sell the rights to another investor OR allow the rights to lapse

Equity Value 8 Nominal value Book value Market value The value assigned to a company’s shares when they are issued. It is an arbitrary amount below $1, often $1, $0.50, $0.10, $0.01. Used in SoFP The book value of shares , the value of a company's equity as reported on the SoFP (TA-TL). To get this per share just divide by the no. of outstanding shares. Current value on the stock market. Statutory requirement, cannot issue new shares if market price drops below this. A measure of the value of the company's assets after all liabilities have been paid, on a per-share basis. Market capitalisation = Mkt price x no. of shares in issue. The difference between the market price at issuance and the nominal value is the share premium.

### Slide 22

TERP The TERP = price at which the shares are EXPECTED TO SETTLE at in a rational market, following the rights issue Its just the weighted average of the pre rights share price and the price at which the new shares are issued Its Theoretical and the actuals share price following the issue will depend on shareholders perception of the RI, the investors hope the actual share price rises above the TERP.
