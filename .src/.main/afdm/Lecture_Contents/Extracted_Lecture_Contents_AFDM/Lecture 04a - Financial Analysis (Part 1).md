### Slide 4

Financial Ratios Express the relationship of one figure in the accounts to another, e.g. Operating Profit to Sales Revenue or Current Assets to Current Liabilities Quick and simple to calculate and understand Ratios allow to evaluate performance and financial health of companies Financial tool allowing time series and cross sectional analysis

---

### Slide 5

Why Use Ratios? Businesses can be of different size and therefore direct comparison can be difficult. By expressing figures in relation to each other, we remove the problem of comparing companies of different size. A few ratios can give us a good basic idea of what is going on in the company.

### Slide 8

Limitations Ratios are backward looking. Creative accounting and Different accounting policies Ratios are only the starting point for further analysis. They help to highlight financial strengths and weaknesses. Ratios are only useful if you can make comparisons – company, industry, year on year Only a detailed analysis of the underlying business/sector/economy will highlight the reasons for the changes in ratios. Restricted vision – don’t forget the non-financial data The accounts do not take into account the effects of inflation

### Slide 11

Financial Ratio Classifications Profitability Ratios, assess a company's ability to generate earnings relative to revenue, assets, equity, and other financial metrics Investment Ratios , help shareholders to understand how the company is managing their investment Efficiency Ratios, evaluate how effectively a company utilises its resources Gearing (Leverage) Ratios , analyse the degree to which a company is financed by debt versus equity. Liquidity Ratios, Measure a company's capacity to meet short-term obligations Understanding these ratios provides insights into a company's operational efficiency, financial stability, and profitability, aiding stakeholders in making informed decisions.

### Slide 19

Investment Ratios Earnings per Share (EPS) This ratio relates the earnings generated by the business, and available to shareholders, during a period, to the number of shares in issue. Considered a fundamental measure of share performance. The trend over time is used to assess the investment potential of shares. x 100 Dividends per Share (DPS) This ratio shows the total amount of dividends declared in the year per ordinary share in issue. It is the dividend per share actually paid in respect of the financial year. Shareholders would normally prefer a higher dividend per share. Price Earnings Ratio. This ratio relates the market value of a share to the earnings per share . It is a valuation ratio expressed in times it is seen a s a predictor of future performance. A higher P/E is suggestive of better potential future success. P/E Calculating historical returns ‘Realised returns’  of stocks Total Shareholder Return takes into account both returns that a shareholder ‘receives’.
