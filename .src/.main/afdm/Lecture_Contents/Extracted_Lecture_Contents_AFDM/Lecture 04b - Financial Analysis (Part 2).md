### Slide 6

Liquidity Liquidity ratios evaluate a company's ability to pay its short-term liabilities . These are commonly used metrics that lenders often look at in the loan approval process. Used along side Gearing ratios to assess the financial risk posed by the level of gearing Current Ratio, measures a company's ability to pay its short-term liabilities using cash, accounts receivable, and inventory Quick Ratio/Acid Test Ratio, measures a company's ability to pay its short-term liabilities using ONLY cash and accounts receivable only. Inventories are excluded since their liquidity is not as good as cash an accounts receivable. This is a conservative measure of liquidity useful when inventory percentage is high. Interest Coverage indicates us how easily a company can pay its interest expenses using its earnings, used by creditors to determine the riskiness of a company's debt. A common view is that the interest coverage ratio should be at least 1.50, otherwise, the company might be struggling paying its debt.

### Slide 13

Efficiency Ratios 13 Average inventories turnover period (days) This ratio measures the average period for which inventories are being held. A business would normally prefer a short turnover period as it often a significant investment for a business. Average settlement period for trade receivables (days) This ratio calculates how long, on average, credit customers take to pay the amounts they owe to a business. A business would normally prefer a shorter period. Except for retailers, selling on credit is the norm. Speed of payment can have a significant effect on cash flow Average settlement period for trade payables This ratio measures how long, on average, a business takes to pay those who have supplied goods and services on credit. Trade payables provide a free source of finance, therefore some businesses attempt to increase this ratio. Beware a loss of goodwill of suppliers Sales revenue to capital employed How effectively the assets of the business are being used to generate sales revenue. Generally, a higher ratio is preferred as this suggests that assets are being used more productively in the generation of revenue. However, a very high ratio may indicate ‘overtrading on its assets’.
