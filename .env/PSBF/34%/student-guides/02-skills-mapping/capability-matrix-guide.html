Capability Matrix Walkthrough Guide

How to complete the Skills/Competence Map Excel template for your Career Development Portfolio (25 marks)

What Is the Capability Matrix?

The Capability Matrix is an Excel workbook with 5 sheets that together build a complete picture of your professional capabilities. You assess yourself against 39 specific capabilities across 7 dimensions, then compare your profile against a real job description and your programme modules. The result is a gap analysis that shows exactly where you need to develop.

Worth 25 marks (25% of portfolio)
Format Excel template (provided in class)
Key to High Marks Connection between skills, growth, and future pathways
Source Framework Adapted from Canadian Management Consultants Competency Framework

Honesty scores higher than inflation. As a Level 4 student, most of your ratings should be 1-2. A few 3s with strong evidence are fine. Ratings of 4 across the board look dishonest and score poorly. Markers reward self-awareness, not self-promotion.

1 Sheet 1: Initial Assessment

A quick first impression. Rate yourself on each of the 7 dimensions overall. Don't overthink this — it's your gut feeling before the detailed assessment.

Example: How to Complete

Dimension Your Rating Why This Rating?
A: Economics Thinking 2 I can apply frameworks like PEST and SWOT but still need guidance on complex analysis
B: Research & Delivery 1 I've written academic reports but haven't done independent research yet
D: Collaboration 3 I led two group projects this semester and resolved a conflict between team members
Tip: Your initial assessment and detailed self-assessment don't need to match perfectly. If they're very different, that's interesting — it shows your detailed analysis revealed things your first impression missed.

2 Sheet 2: Self-Assessment (The Core Sheet)

This is where most of your work happens. Rate all 39 capabilities AND write a justification for each rating. The justification is what earns marks — not the number.

Strong Self-Assessment Examples

A3: Strategic Analysis Rating: 2
Strong justification: "Applied Porter's Five Forces and PEST analysis to evaluate Vietcombank's competitive position in the NEU6021MKT midterm. Identified 3 strategic gaps (digital banking, rural penetration, ESG compliance) and proposed recommendations. Still developing ability to connect multiple frameworks — my SWOT integration was surface-level."
D2: Leadership Rating: 2
Strong justification: "Took the coordinator role in my Business Strategy group (5 members). Created a task allocation spreadsheet and scheduled weekly check-ins. Struggled when two members missed deadlines — I covered their work instead of addressing the issue, which I now recognise as a leadership gap."
B4: Presentation Skills Rating: 1
Weak justification: "I am quite good at presentations. I have done many presentations in class."

What Makes a Good Justification?

Strong (Specific + Evidence) Weak (Vague + No Evidence)
"Used Excel to build a DCF model for the Vinamilk case study in Financial Economics" "I know how to use Excel well"
"Received feedback that my essay lacked critical depth — revised using the evaluation framework from PD5" "I can handle feedback"
"Rating 0 — I haven't taken any modules covering econometric methods yet" "Rating 3" (with no justification for why it's high)
Tip: A rating of 0 or 1 with honest justification scores HIGHER than a rating of 3 with no evidence. The rubric rewards "strong self-awareness" and "explicit connections" — not high numbers.

3 Sheet 3: Job Description Assessment

Score the same 39 capabilities, but this time from the perspective of a real job. In class, we use the GES Assistant Economist Graduate Scheme 2026. Ask: "How important is this capability for THIS job?"

The GES Assistant Economist Role Values:

Key Competency Area Likely Rating Why
Making Effective Decisions 4 Core assessment criterion — must analyse data and brief policy-makers
Working Together 3 Cross-departmental collaboration expected but not the primary focus
Delivering at Pace 4 Tight turnaround on policy briefs and ministerial submissions
Quantitative Methods 4 Economics degree at 2:1+ required; statistical analysis is daily work
Creative/Visual Design 1 Not a primary requirement for this role
Tip: Read the full job description carefully. Don't guess — use the actual requirements, essential criteria, and desirable criteria to inform your ratings. Different jobs have very different profiles.

4 Sheet 4: Assessment of Your Programme

For each of the 39 capabilities, identify which Level 4 modules are developing that capability and how. This shows you understand where your learning is happening.

Example Entries

Capability Module(s) How It's Developed
A3: Strategic Analysis NEU6021MKT Case study analysis using Porter's, PEST, SWOT in Responsible Business Strategy
A1-2: Data Analysis NEU6021ECN Excel-based quantitative exercises in Quantitative Methods for Economics
D1: Teamwork NEU6021MKT, UMET9U-30-1 Group presentations in Business Strategy; paperclip market simulation in PD5
E1: Career Planning UMET9U-30-1 Odyssey Plans and capability matrix in Professional Skills PD workshops

Tip: Don't forget non-module sources: part-time work, student clubs, volunteering, self-study. If you're developing cross-cultural collaboration by studying in English as a Vietnamese student, that counts and it's a significant professional competence.

Your gap analysis must connect to your Odyssey Plans: if the GES job needs data analysis at 4 and you're at 2, your Odyssey Plan should show WHEN and HOW you'll bridge that gap (e.g., "Year 2: Complete econometrics module + Excel VBA self-study").

Connecting Your Matrix to Your Odyssey Plans

Your capability matrix and Odyssey Plans must tell a coherent story. The matrix identifies WHERE you are; the Odyssey Plan shows WHERE you're going and HOW you'll get there.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Don't Do This Do This Instead
Rate everything 3 or 4 with no justification Be honest — mostly 1-2 at Level 4 with specific evidence for each
Write "I am good at this" as justification Give a concrete example: "Applied PEST to Vietcombank case in NEU6021MKT"
Leave Sheet 4 (Programme) mostly empty Think carefully about every module — most develop multiple capabilities
Ignore gaps and only highlight strengths Gaps are valuable — they show self-awareness and drive your development plan
Complete the matrix without looking at your Odyssey Plans Cross-reference: your gaps should appear as development milestones in your Plans
Only include academic skills Include part-time work, volunteering, clubs, bilingual ability, cross-cultural skills
Copy someone else's justifications Your evidence must be YOUR experiences — generic text will be flagged

Pre-Submission Checklist

Before submitting your Skills/Competence Map, verify every item:

  • All 5 sheets completed (Initial Assessment, Self-Assessment, Job Description, Programme, Results)
  • All 39 capabilities have a rating AND a written justification
  • Ratings are honest and realistic (mostly 1-2, not all 3s and 4s)
  • Every justification includes a specific, concrete example from YOUR experience
  • Job Description sheet is scored against the GES Assistant Economist role (or another real job)
  • Programme sheet maps capabilities to specific Level 4 modules with explanations
  • Capabilities from extra-curricular activities are included (work, clubs, volunteering)
  • Gap analysis connects to your Odyssey Plan milestones
  • Results sheet shows meaningful comparisons (not all identical scores)
  • File saved as Skills_Map_[YourName].xlsx
