Evidence Document: Annotated Examples

500-word assessment of your current skills, attitudes, and work-life balance preferences — the evidence supporting your Odyssey Plans

What the Evidence Document Does

The Evidence Document is a 500-word assessment of your current skills, attitudes, and work-life balance preferences that supports your career plans. It answers one question: "Why do these career plans make sense for THIS specific person?"

Section ~Words Content Source Exercise
Skills & Attitudes ~200 Your current skills self-assessment and attitudes (Workview, Lifeview, core values — what drives you and what you can do) PD2 exercises, Skills Map
Work-Life Balance ~100 Your work-life balance preferences and what they reveal about your priorities PD2 dashboard
Connection to Plans ~200 How your skills, attitudes, and balance preferences explain WHY you chose Plan A and Plan B Links to Odyssey Plans

70%+ Example Strong Evidence Document

Student: Nguyen Minh Anh (same student as the Odyssey Plan examples) | Word count: 498

Strong Example — Annotated 498 words

Workview & Lifeview (~150 words)
My Workview centres on the belief that work should contribute to something beyond personal gain. Growing up watching my parents run a small family business in Hanoi, I saw that work provides not only financial security but also a sense of purpose and connection to community. For me, a meaningful career is one where my daily effort contributes to Vietnam's economic development, not just my own advancement.

My Lifeview reinforces this: I find meaning through relationships and through seeing tangible impact from my efforts. I value stability — not because I fear change, but because stable foundations allow me to take calculated risks. There is a tension here: I want both security and impact, but the most impactful roles (startups, development finance) often come with less security. This tension directly shapes why I have two genuinely different career plans.

Core Values (~100 words)
My top five values, ranked through the PD2 forced-ranking exercise, are: Family, Contribution, Integrity, Learning, and Stability. Family ranks first because my career decisions are inseparable from my responsibility to my parents and younger sister. Contribution matters because I want my economics knowledge to improve how Vietnamese businesses access capital. Integrity means I would not pursue a role that conflicts with my ethical standards, even if it paid well. Learning reflects my belief that career satisfaction comes from continuous growth. Stability is last among my top five — important, but not at the expense of meaningful work.

Work-Life Balance (~100 words)
My current Work-Life Balance dashboard scores are: Work 7/10, Play 3/10, Love 7/10, Health 5/10. The imbalance is clear: I have invested heavily in academics and family relationships at the expense of leisure and physical wellbeing. This pattern concerns me because it is unsustainable over a 40-year career. Both of my career plans must therefore include deliberate strategies for maintaining health and personal interests — Plan A's structured corporate environment may help with this; Plan B's startup intensity may make it harder.

Connection to Plans (~150 words)
Plan A (corporate banking at a Vietnamese commercial bank) directly serves my top values of Family and Stability while allowing me to contribute to Vietnam's financial infrastructure. The structured career path offers the security my family expects, and the client-facing role means I can see the tangible impact of my work on businesses accessing capital. My Workview — work as contribution — aligns with banking's role in economic development.

Plan B (FinTech startup) prioritises my values of Contribution and Learning over Stability. My Lifeview tension — wanting both security and impact — is why Plan B exists: if the corporate path feels too constrained, entrepreneurship lets me maximise impact, even at higher personal risk. My capability matrix gap in technical skills (data analysis: 1/4) shapes Stage 1 of Plan B, where I prioritise Python and fintech skills development before launching anything.

Personal & Specific Opens with a concrete personal experience (parents' business), not generic career advice. Could only be written by this student.
Honest Tension Acknowledges a real conflict between security and impact. This shows self-awareness — the rubric's key differentiator for 70%+.
Values Are Ranked Doesn't just list values — explains the ranking and WHY each matters. "Stability is last" is a brave, honest statement.
Dashboard = Evidence Gives actual scores (7, 3, 7, 5) and interprets what the pattern reveals. Connects imbalance to career plan requirements.
Forward-Looking Doesn't just describe the imbalance — says both plans must address it. This is design thinking in action.
Direct Connection Each plan is explicitly linked to specific values. "Plan A serves Family and Stability" — clear, traceable logic.
Cross-References Matrix Mentions the capability matrix gap (data analysis: 1/4) to show how the Skills Map connects to Plan B. Coherence across components.

40-49% Example Weak Evidence Document

This example shows common mistakes. Compare it to the strong version above.

Weak Example — Annotated 312 words (under minimum)

Workview & Lifeview
I believe that work is important for everyone. Work gives us money and helps us to have a good life. I want to have a career that is fulfilling and allows me to support my family. I think life is about being happy and having good relationships with people around you.

I value work-life balance and want a career that lets me have time for my hobbies and family. I think it is important to find a job that you enjoy because you spend most of your time working.

Core Values
My top values are: happiness, family, success, money, and health. These values are important to me because they help me to live a good life. I want to be happy in my career and successful in my work.

Work-Life Balance
I think my work-life balance is okay. Sometimes I study too much but I also have time for friends. I need to exercise more.

Connection to Plans
My career plans match my values because I want a job that pays well and makes me happy. Plan A is banking because it has good salary and career progression. Plan B is also in finance because I am studying finance. Both plans will help me achieve my goals.

Generic "Work is important for everyone" could be written by any student on any programme. No personal insight at all.
No PD2 Exercises No evidence of having completed the Workview or Lifeview reflections. No specific beliefs about work or life.
Values Not Ranked Lists 5 values but doesn't explain the ranking, why they matter, or how they were chosen. "Happiness" and "success" are vague.
No Dashboard Scores No actual Work-Life Balance scores (should be Work: X/10, Play: X/10, etc.). "Okay" is not an assessment.
No Real Connection "Pays well and makes me happy" doesn't connect values to specific plan choices. Plan B is "also in finance" — not a genuine alternative.
Under Word Count 312 words — well below the 500-word target. Missing depth in every section.

Why the Strong Version Scores 70%+

Rubric Criterion Strong Example Weak Example
Design thinking evidence Identifies a tension (security vs impact) and uses it to explain why two different plans exist. Iterative, reflective thinking. "I want a job that pays well and makes me happy" — no design thinking process evident.
Self-awareness Honest about imbalances (Play: 3/10), acknowledges values ranking isn't comfortable (Stability is last). "My work-life balance is okay" — no honest assessment or discomfort.
Specificity Names parents' business, specific dashboard scores (7,3,7,5), specific capability matrix rating (1/4). No specific experiences, no numbers, no concrete examples.
Connection to plans Each plan explicitly mapped to specific values. Plan B explained through the Lifeview tension. "Both plans will help me achieve my goals" — no specific connection.
Coherence Cross-references capability matrix, dashboard scores, and values throughout. No reference to any other portfolio component.
The authenticity test: Could this document have been written by any student without doing the PD2 exercises? The strong version clearly could not — it references specific scores, personal tensions, and family circumstances. The weak version could have been written in 10 minutes by anyone.

Writing Tips

Instead of This... Write This...
"I value work-life balance" "My Play score of 3/10 reveals I have neglected leisure — both plans must address this"
"I want a fulfilling career" "My Workview reveals I define fulfilment as contributing to Vietnam's economic development"
"My values are important to me" "Family ranks first because my career decisions are inseparable from my responsibility to my parents"
"I chose banking because it's a good career" "Plan A serves my top values of Family and Stability while allowing Contribution through client-facing corporate banking"
"Both plans match my values" "Plan A prioritises Stability; Plan B prioritises Contribution and Learning — reflecting the genuine tension in my Lifeview"
