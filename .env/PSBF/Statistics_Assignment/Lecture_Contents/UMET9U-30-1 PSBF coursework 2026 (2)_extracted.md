Assessment Instructions

Stock returns are leading indicators for output growth. Financial variables react quickly to new information. It takes more time for macroeconomic variables to react to new situations. It has been studied how previous year’s stock returns can be used to predict economic growth of the following year. Mauro (2003) has studied the correlation between lagged stock returns and output growth for a cross-section of emerging and developed economies (Mauro, P., 2003. Stock returns and output growth in emerging and advanced economies. Journal of Development Economics, 71(1), pp.129-153.). Mauro (2003) has two parts. The first part analyses for which countries the correlation is significant. The second part tries to find a number of characteristics which determine the significance. Do emerging economies behave differently than developed economies?

Specifically, using data provided on Blackboard, you are required to: 

Give a short introduction to the relationship between stock returns and economic growth. 

Compute descriptive statistics for stock returns and economic growth. Discuss relevant information from these descriptive statistics.

Analyse the distributional properties of these variables with the help of a histogram and a box plot. 

Study the relationship between lagged stock returns and economic growth with the help of a correlation coefficient and a scatter graph.

Estimate a regression model with economic growth as dependent variable and lagged stock returns as explanatory variable. Interpret the slope coefficient, perform a t test to study whether the relationship is statistically significant and discuss the coefficient of determination R2. Perform a further regression model where additionally a lagged dependent variable is included as a robustness analysis.

Give a short summary of your findings.  

The above should be presented in a statistics report of no more than 1,500 words, including an introduction and conclusion, with any relevant references collected in a reference list.
